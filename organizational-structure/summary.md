# Youth Congress Nepal - Organizational Structure

## Overview

Youth Congress Nepal is organized in a hierarchical structure with three main levels of committees, each with specific roles, positions, and departments.

---

## Committee Levels

### 1. Central Committee
The highest level of organizational structure, responsible for national-level decisions and coordination.

**Current Status (Second Term - 2025 onwards):**
- Has elected positions (President, General Secretary, Deputy General Secretary, Treasurer, Committee Members)
- Fully operational with complete organizational structure

### 2. Provincial Committee
Operates at the provincial level, coordinating activities within each of Nepal's 7 provinces.

**Current Status (Second Term - 2025 onwards):**
- Currently has temporary positions (Coordinator, Vice Coordinator, Members)
- Elections pending - will transition to elected positions after elections

### 3. District Committee
Operates at the district level, managing activities within each of Nepal's 77 districts.

**Current Status (Second Term - 2025 onwards):**
- Currently has temporary positions (Coordinator, Vice Coordinator, Members)
- Elections pending - will transition to elected positions after elections

---

## Terms

### Term Structure
- **First Term:** 2017 - 2024
- **Second Term:** 2025 onwards (Current)

**Note:** After District and Provincial elections are completed, a new Central Committee will be set up for the next term.

---

## Position Structure

### Temporary Positions (Before Election or Temporary Appointments)
Currently used in Provincial and District Committees (Second Term - 2025 onwards):

1. **Coordinator** - Leads the committee temporarily
2. **Vice Coordinator** - Assists the coordinator
3. **Members** - General committee members

### Elected Positions (After Elections)
Currently active in Central Committee (Second Term - 2025 onwards). Will be implemented in Provincial and District Committees after elections:

1. **President** - Chief executive of the committee
2. **General Secretary** - Handles administrative and secretarial duties
3. **Deputy General Secretary** - Assists the General Secretary
4. **Treasurer** - Manages financial affairs
5. **Committee Members** - Elected representatives

---

## Departments

Each committee (Central, Provincial, and District) includes the following departments:

1. **Policy, Research and Training Department**
   - Develops policies and conducts research
   - Organizes training programs

2. **International Relations and Diplomacy Department**
   - Manages international partnerships
   - Handles diplomatic relations

3. **Education, Health and Sports Department**
   - Focuses on educational initiatives
   - Promotes health and sports activities

4. **Human Rights, Parliamentary Affairs and Legal Department**
   - Advocates for human rights
   - Handles legal and parliamentary matters

5. **Environmental and Sustainability Department**
   - Promotes environmental awareness
   - Implements sustainability initiatives

6. **Economic Affairs Department**
   - Addresses economic issues
   - Promotes economic development

7. **Social Welfare and Inclusion Department**
   - Works on social welfare programs
   - Promotes inclusion and diversity

8. **Information, Communication and Publicity Department**
   - Manages communication channels
   - Handles publicity and media relations

9. **Organization, Coordination and Youth Engagement Department**
   - Coordinates organizational activities
   - Engages with youth communities

### Department Positions
1. **Department Lead** - Heads the department
2. **Department Co-Lead** - Assists the department lead
3. **Department Members** - Active members of the department

---

## Special Committees

### 1. Accounts Committee
- Oversees financial matters
- Ensures financial transparency and accountability

### 2. Discipline Committee
- Maintains organizational discipline
- Handles disciplinary matters

### Committee Position
- **Committee Lead** - Heads the special committee

## Position Assignment Rules and Scenarios

### Multiple Positions per Member

Members can hold multiple positions simultaneously across different levels and categories:

#### Scenario 1: Cross-Level Positions
- A **Central Committee Member** can also be a **Department Lead** in the Central Committee
- A **District Committee Member** can hold a **Department Position** in a **Provincial Committee**
- Example: A member can be "District Committee Member" in Kathmandu District AND "Department Lead of Communication Department" in Bagmati Provincial Committee

#### Scenario 2: Multiple Terms
- A member can hold the same position across multiple terms
- Example: A member can be "Committee Member" in First Term (2017-2024) AND "Committee Member" in Second Term (2025 onwards)

#### Scenario 3: Multiple Positions in Same Term
- A member can hold multiple positions in the same term
- Example: A member can be "Central Committee Member" AND "Department Lead of Policy Department" AND "Accounts Committee Lead" all in the same term

#### Scenario 4: Cross-Category Positions
- A member can hold positions in different categories simultaneously
- Example: A member can be "Committee Member" (committee category) AND "Department Lead" (department category) AND "Accounts Committee Lead" (special committee category)

### Position Hierarchy and Access

- Positions are tracked independently - each position is a separate record
- A member's access and responsibilities are determined by the combination of all their active positions
- Positions can span across different committee levels, categories, and terms
- The system supports flexible position assignments to accommodate organizational needs

## Notes

- **Central Committee** currently operates with elected positions (Second Term - 2025 onwards)
- **Provincial and District Committees** currently operate with temporary positions (Coordinator, Vice Coordinator, Members)
- After elections, Provincial and District Committees will transition to elected positions (President, General Secretary, etc.)
- After District and Provincial elections are completed, a new Central Committee will be set up
- Each level (Central, Provincial, District) maintains the same organizational structure
- Departments exist at all three levels
- Special committees (Accounts and Discipline) operate at all levels
- All committees follow the same department and position structure for consistency
- **Members can hold multiple positions** across different levels, categories, and terms simultaneously
- Position assignments are flexible to accommodate organizational needs and member capabilities
